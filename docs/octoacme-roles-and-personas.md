# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers ensure quality standards of each release are met through comprehensive testing strategies. They collaborate with Developers and Product Managers to define test plans, conduct manual and automated testing, and verify acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans for features and releases
- Conduct manual and automated testing across the application
- Verify acceptance criteria are met before release
- Report and track defects through resolution
- Maintain test automation frameworks and scripts
- Participate in defining quality standards and DoD (Definition of Done)

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal production defects
- Increase test coverage and automation
- Reduce time-to-detection for bugs and regressions

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups with development team
- Test reports and quality metrics in sprint reviews
- Collaboration with Developers on test coverage and bug triage
- Coordination with Product Managers on acceptance criteria validation
- Release readiness reports to Release Managers

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers partner with Product Managers and Developers to define user flows and design interfaces. They ensure deliverables meet user experience standards, adopt and incorporate user feedback, and create design systems and style guides.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes for new features
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Develop and maintain design systems and style guides
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Gather and incorporate user feedback into design iterations
- Ensure accessibility and responsive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-friendly interfaces
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Improve user satisfaction and engagement metrics

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and feedback reports
- Design specifications and style guide updates
- Collaboration with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Feedback sessions with Support Leads on usability issues

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate the deployment process, track release schedules, and manage cross-team communication during releases. They ensure smooth and safe deployments by working with Project Managers, QA Engineers, and Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules across teams
- Manage deployment workflows and automation
- Track release readiness and sign-offs from stakeholders
- Own rollback procedures and deployment verification
- Communicate release status to stakeholders and support teams
- Maintain release documentation and post-mortems

### Goals
- Achieve zero-downtime deployments
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Improve deployment frequency and reliability

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Project Managers and development leads
- Go/no-go decisions with QA Engineers and Developers
- Deployment status updates to stakeholders
- Post-deployment reports and metrics
- Coordination with Support Leads on customer communications

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads act as the bridge between end-users/customers and development teams. They ensure customer feedback, incidents, and feature requests are fed back into planning and improvements, while tracking support metrics and escalating critical issues.

### Responsibilities
- Manage customer support tickets and escalations
- Track and analyze support metrics and trends
- Communicate customer pain points to Product and Development teams
- Provide input for product prioritization based on customer needs
- Document known issues and workarounds
- Coordinate with teams during incidents and outages

### Goals
- Maintain high customer satisfaction scores
- Reduce mean time to resolution (MTTR) for support issues
- Ensure customer voice influences product decisions

### Typical Communication
- Weekly syncs with Product Managers on feedback and feature requests
- Bug reports and clarifications with Developers
- Usability feedback sessions with UX/UI Designers
- Incident updates during outages and critical issues
- Support metrics and trend reports to leadership
